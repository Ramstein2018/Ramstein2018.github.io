Immigration Physical Examination Process

An immigration physical is a medical examination required by the U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS) for individuals seeking certain immigration benefits, such as adjustment of status, a green card, or a visa. Here's what the process typically entails and what a patient should do prior to the examination:

1. Medical History Review
The physician will review the patient's medical history, including any previous medical conditions, surgeries, or vaccinations.
2. Vaccination Status
The physician will verify that the patient has received all required vaccinations according to the U.S. vaccination guidelines.
3. Physical Examination
A thorough physical examination will be conducted, which may include checking vital signs, examining the eyes, ears, nose, throat, heart, lungs, abdomen, skin, and musculoskeletal system.
4. Tuberculosis (TB) Screening
A tuberculin skin test (TST) or an interferon-gamma release assay (IGRA) blood test will be performed to screen for tuberculosis.
5. Blood Tests
A blood test may be required to check for certain infectious diseases, such as syphilis and HIV.
6. Vaccinations (if necessary)
If the patient is missing any required vaccinations, they may be administered during the examination.
7. Chest X-ray (if necessary)
If there are any indications of a possible tuberculosis infection, a chest X-ray may be ordered for further evaluation.
8. Completion of Form I-693
The physician will complete and seal Form I-693, which is the official USCIS Report of Medical Examination and Vaccination Record.
